Privacy Policy — Alchohalt

Effective date: 2026-07-05 Last updated: 2026-07-05

TL;DR

What you log stays on your phone by default. We don't run a server that sees your drink log, mood, or journal entries. The only third parties that ever see anything from your use of Alchohalt are:

  1. Apple App Store / Google Play — if and when in-app purchases are

offered, they handle your subscription payment. They would tell us only "this anonymous user paid." They don't tell us your name, card, or anything else. This build ships with in-app purchases disabled, so no purchase path is active today.

  1. RevenueCat (purchase-validation broker) — if and when in-app

purchases are offered, they would receive an anonymous user ID and a product ID at the moment you make a purchase, so we can confirm your premium entitlement is real. They never see what you log.

  1. Anthropic — only if you opt in to AI Insights. AI Insights is

off by default. When you turn it on, an anonymized summary of your patterns (drink counts grouped by week, mood-tag counts, HALT counts, streak length, day-of-week pattern) is sent through a server-side proxy to Anthropic. Your raw data — journal text, notes, exact times, custom drink names — never leaves the device, even with AI Insights on. See Section 5 for the full data flow. Everything else — every drink you log, every mood tag, every HALT trigger, every dollar spent, every journal entry — stays on your device unless you (a) explicitly export it, or (b) opt in to AI Insights.

There is no account, no sign-up, and no email — the app has no server that could receive your entries.

1. What we collect

On your device only (not transmitted anywhere)

  • Drink log entries (timestamp, type, amount, cost, intention, craving,

HALT flags)

  • Goals (daily/weekly limits, monthly budget)
  • Journal entries (text + emoji + mood tag)
  • Streak counts and statistics
  • App preferences (language, theme, reminder settings)

This data lives in your device's local storage (Capacitor Preferences API on Android / iOS, IndexedDB / localStorage on web). It is not encrypted at rest — it relies on your device's own protections (your device screen lock and OS sandboxing). We have no remote copy. We cannot recover it if you uninstall the app.

Files you export are controlled by you

  • JSON export (free): a plain-text .json file you save wherever

you choose. It is not encrypted — it carries a checksum for integrity only, not confidentiality. Treat it like any sensitive file.

  • Encrypted backup (premium, .alch-backup): encrypted on your

device with a passphrase you choose (Argon2id key derivation + XChaCha20-Poly1305). We never see the passphrase or the contents.

Both exports produce a file you hold. The app has no account and no server, so there is nothing else to "back up to" — your only off-device copies are the files you choose to make.

2. How we use what we collect

When in-app purchases are enabled, the purchase-token data flowing through Apple/Google/RevenueCat is used only to:

  • Verify that your premium subscription is active when you open the app
  • Restore your premium access if you delete and reinstall the app

This build ships with in-app purchases disabled, so no purchase path is active today.

We do not sell, share, rent, or otherwise transfer your data to anyone else. We have no advertising partners.

3. Data retention

  • On your device: retained until you delete the app or use the

"Wipe all data" button in Settings.

  • Apple / Google: retained per their respective privacy policies.
  • RevenueCat: retained per their privacy policy

(https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy). Typically the purchase record is kept for the lifetime of your subscription plus 7 years for accounting. We operate no server that holds your personal data. The optional AI Insights proxy, if you ever enable it, keeps only an anonymous rate-limit log (a rotating anonymous ID + timestamps — no entries, no identity, no prompt content); see Section 5. In v1.0 that proxy is not even deployed, so nothing is retained off-device at all.

4. Your rights (GDPR / UK GDPR / CCPA-CPRA / VCDPA / CPA / CTDPA)

Under applicable data-protection laws you have rights to access, port, correct, and erase your data, and to be free from discrimination for exercising them. Because, by default, all of your data is on your own device:

  • Access / portability: open Settings → "Export data (JSON)" or

use the encrypted backup feature (premium).

  • Correction: edit any entry directly in the app.
  • Erasure: Settings → "Wipe all data" (double-confirm). Because

your data lives only on this device, this removes every copy we could ever reach. If you saved an encrypted backup file, delete that file too — it is the only other copy, and only you hold it.

  • Opt out of "sale" / "sharing" / targeted advertising: there is

nothing to opt out of — we do not sell or share your data and we run no advertising. (California CCPA/CPRA, Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA.)

For the purchase-token slice held by Apple/Google/RevenueCat, those parties are the relevant data controllers/processors for those records; you can also contact them directly.

If you reside in the EU/EEA, the UK, California, Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, or another jurisdiction granting additional rights, those rights apply. To exercise any right, email the address in Section 8 — note that for on-device data we have nothing to retrieve on our side, so the fastest path is the in-app Export / Wipe controls above. We do not charge a fee for, or discriminate against, exercising these rights.

Residents of Washington, Nevada, Colorado, and Connecticut have additional consumer-health-data rights — see the Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy.

5. Optional features that change this posture

Crash reporting — OFF by default

The app contains an opt-in crash reporter. It is off by default. In the current release it is additionally a no-op: no crash-reporting endpoint is configured in the build, so nothing is transmitted even if you opt in. If a future release configures an endpoint, enabling crash reporting (opt-in) would send only sanitized crash metadata — error message, stack trace (file + line), OS family, app version, and timestamp. Your wellness data — drink entries, mood, journal, notes, tags — is NEVER included in a crash report. You can disable it from Settings at any time.

Encrypted backup — a file you create and hold

You can export an encrypted backup file (.alch-backup) from Settings → Privacy & data. It is sealed on your device (Argon2id key derivation + XChaCha20-Poly1305) with a passphrase you choose, and the app simply writes it to wherever you point it — your own device storage, your own cloud drive, or an email to yourself. The app never uploads it and we never receive it. Without your passphrase nobody — including us — can read it. There is no account, no server, and no sync service: this exported file is the only way your data can exist off the device, and you are the only one who holds it.

AI Insights — OFF by default, opt-in only

AI Insights is an optional, opt-in feature available on premium tiers. It is off by default. Turning it on requires:

  1. A premium subscription (so the feature is gated by entitlement).
  2. Reading the in-app consent screen that lists exactly what is sent

and what is never sent.

  1. Affirming the "I understand" checkbox.
  2. Tapping Enable AI Insights.

What gets sent (only after you opt in)

When you tap "Generate insights," an anonymized summary is built locally on your device and sent through our server-side proxy to Anthropic (Claude). The summary contains exclusively:

  • Drink counts grouped by ISO week (no exact timestamps).
  • Mood-tag counts (e.g., happy: 12, anxious: 4).
  • HALT trigger counts (hungry / angry / lonely / tired).
  • Intention counts (celebrate / social / taste / bored / cope / other).
  • Day-of-week pattern (counts only).
  • Current streak length.
  • An anonymous device ID generated locally at consent (rotates on

revoke + re-grant).

  • Your locale (e.g., en).

The exact JSON shape is defined in src/lib/ai/types.ts as SanitizedAIPayload. The sanitization function in src/lib/ai/sanitize.ts is allowlist-based — fields outside this list cannot pass through. A defense-in-depth check runs before every send and refuses to transmit if any forbidden field is present.

What is NEVER sent, even with AI Insights on

  • Your name, email, phone, or address (we never collect these).
  • Your IP address beyond what TLS unavoidably exposes to our proxy.
  • Your location or any GPS data.
  • Any free text you've written: journal entries, drink notes,

alt-action text.

  • Custom drink names you've created (these may contain personal

references like "Dad's IPA").

  • Your weight, sex, or any other profile field.
  • Exact timestamps of any logged event.
  • Any persistent device identifier other than the rotating

AI-Insights anonymous instance ID.

Provider posture (Anthropic)

  • Anthropic does not train its production models on customer data.
  • Prompts may be retained for up to 30 days for abuse detection.
  • See https://www.anthropic.com/legal/privacy.

How the proxy works

Alchohalt itself does not embed an AI provider API key in the app bundle. The opt-in network call goes to a server-side proxy that holds the API key and rate-limits per anonymous device ID. We log only the timestamp and the anonymous ID for rate-limiting; we do not log the prompt body. The proxy retains nothing else about your usage.

In v1.0 the proxy is not yet deployed; the consent flow is live but the network call itself is gated behind a feature flag and returns a "coming in v1.1" placeholder. No data leaves the device until the proxy ships.

Revoking consent

Settings → AI → Revoke consent. Revoking:

  • Wipes the anonymous device ID locally.
  • Stops any in-flight request immediately (client-side abort).
  • Future re-grants generate a fresh anonymous device ID, so the

proxy cannot link before-vs-after-revoke usage.

There is nothing for us to delete on the server side beyond the proxy's rate-limit log; that log retains only the anonymous ID and timestamps, not your prompt content.

Your state-specific rights

If you reside in Washington (MHMDA), Nevada (SB 370), Colorado (CPA), or Connecticut (CTDPA), additional consumer-health-data rights apply. See Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy for the state-specific disclosures.

6. Children

Alchohalt is rated 17+ on the App Store and Mature 17+ on Google Play because it tracks alcohol consumption. It is not directed at, or intended for, children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 17. Because the app has no general account system and stores data on-device, we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13 within the meaning of the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) — there is no server-side record for us to hold. If a child has used the app, the data is on that device only; uninstalling the app or using "Wipe all data" removes it.

7. Not medical advice

Alchohalt is a self-tracking tool, not treatment for alcohol use disorder, and it is not a substitute for professional medical care. Alcohol withdrawal can be medically dangerous — if you are a heavy drinker considering quitting or sharply cutting down, please talk to a doctor first. If you are in a medical emergency or need urgent help with substance use, contact emergency services or a healthcare professional right now.

8. Changes to this policy

When we change this policy, we'll show a one-time in-app banner the next time you open the app, with a summary of what changed. The current version is always available inside the app at Settings → Legal, and at the public legal URL referenced by the App Store / Google Play listing. The "Last updated" date at the top of this document reflects the most recent change.
